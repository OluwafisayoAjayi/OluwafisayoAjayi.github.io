Research

My research is in energy economics, environmental economics, housing insecurity, poverty, inequality, and applied microeconometrics. I study how energy affordability, environmental conditions, and policy choices shape household vulnerability, especially among low-income and vulnerable communities.

My current work examines the relationship between energy burden and eviction filing rates, the housing-market effects of oil price shocks, and broader questions at the intersection of energy policy, environmental exposure, and economic inequality.

About

I am a PhD student in Resource Economics at the University of Massachusetts Amherst. My research interests are in energy economics, environmental economics, housing insecurity, poverty, inequality, and applied microeconometrics.

My work examines how energy affordability, environmental conditions, and policy choices shape household vulnerability. I am especially interested in how energy burden, environmental exposure, and housing insecurity affect low-income and vulnerable communities.

Before joining UMass Amherst, I completed my master's training in economics at the University of Maine, where my research focused on the relationship between energy burden and eviction filing rates. My broader goal is to use applied economic research to study policies that can reduce energy poverty, housing instability, and environmental inequality.

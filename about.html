About

I am a PhD student in Resource Economics at the University of Massachusetts Amherst. My research interests are in energy economics, environmental economics, and applied microeconometrics.

My work examines how energy and environmental conditions shape household well-being, especially for vulnerable communities. I am particularly interested in using data-driven and causal methods to study questions related to energy affordability, housing stability, environmental exposure, and economic inequality.

Outside research, I enjoy building accessible data dashboards that make economic and environmental issues easier to understand.
